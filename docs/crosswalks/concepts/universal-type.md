## Scope Note

**Selection rationale (per `ADR-0018`'s admission bar):** Universal / Type is the sixth concept drafted from the `0.2.0` batch. Direct primary-source verification this session found a nameable category in **3 of 8 sources** (GFO `Category`/`Universal`, SUMO `SetOrClass`/`Set`/`Class`, UFO's rigidity-based type taxonomy), meeting `TODO.md`'s own 3-source seed count exactly. Unlike Situation/State of Affairs (the previous concept drafted, where the cross-source evidence was thinner than the seed implied), this concept's UFO evidence turned out to be *stronger* than the seed suggested: the full `kind`/`subkind`/`phase`/`role`/`category`/`phaseMixin`/`roleMixin`/`mixin` rigidity taxonomy is directly quotable from UFO's own 2021 comprehensive paper, not only from the derived OntoUML vocabulary (contrast `situation-state-of-affairs.md`'s `note-002`, where the equivalent UFO evidence came *only* from OntoUML). BFO, DOLCE, YAMATO, TUpper, and GUM were confirmed to have no reified Universal/Type/Class category.

This crosswalk compares each source ontology's own treatment of Universal/Type as a top-level category — an entity whose instances share it as a common classifier, as distinct from the particular/individual entities that instantiate it — not any single source's full theory of types (UFO's rigidity-based rich typology, GFO's realist immanent-universals theory, and SUMO's set-theoretic Class/Set distinction are each larger literatures than what is scoped here). Sources compared: BFO, DOLCE, SUMO, UFO, GFO, YAMATO, TUpper, GUM (per `ADR-0015`'s 8-source scope).

**Cross-cutting finding, established this session:** GFO is the only source to make Universal/Category one half of a top-level, formally exhaustive split of *everything* (`Item` = `Category` ∪ `Individual`, mutually disjoint) — the same structural pattern already found for GFO's `Abstract`/`Concrete`/`Space_time` split in `abstract-concrete.md`. SUMO instead files its `SetOrClass` as a subclass of `Abstract` (a peer of `Quantity`/`Attribute`/`Relation`/`Proposition`, per `abstract-concrete.md`'s `note-004`), so the "class of things" is itself just one of several abstract sub-kinds, not a top-level split. UFO's treatment is the richest and most distinctive: rather than a single Universal/Type class, UFO subdivides types by two orthogonal criteria — rigidity (does the type apply necessarily or contingently to its instances?) and sortality (does the type supply an identity/individuation principle, or only common properties across multiple identity-bearing kinds?) — yielding `kind`/`subkind` (rigid sortals), `phase`/`role` (anti-rigid sortals, intrinsic vs. relational), and `category`/`phaseMixin`/`roleMixin`/`mixin` (non-sortals of varying rigidity). No other source in this crosswalk makes a comparable rigidity distinction. See `note-001`, `note-002`.

Exclusions: this crosswalk does not address Situation/State of Affairs (already drafted, `situation-state-of-affairs.md`; UFO's OntoUML vocabulary is a shared primary source between the two crosswalks, noted where relevant) or Mereology/Parthood/Aggregate (a separate `0.2.0`-batch candidate; UFO's `Collective` — a non-quantity, non-object leaf of `Substantial` — is directly relevant background for that future crosswalk, not covered here).

**Core.ttl placement is deliberately left open at this `draft` stage**, for the same reasons as the prior five `0.2.0`-batch crosswalks — see Uncertainty.
