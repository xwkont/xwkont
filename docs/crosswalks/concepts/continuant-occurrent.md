## Scope Note

This crosswalk compares the top-level temporal-persistence split found in BFO (continuant/occurrent), DOLCE (endurant/perdurant), and UFO (endurant/perdurant), and a structurally different but often-compared analog in SUMO (Object/Process under a Physical/Abstract partition). It is XwkOnt's first concept crosswalk, chosen deliberately over a more concrete concept (e.g., Object) because it is the most abstract split each source ontology makes, is the best-evidenced in existing comparative literature, and directly validates XwkOnt's own top-level scaffold (`xwkont-core:Continuant`, `xwkont-core:Occurrent` in `data/ontology/core.ttl`) against real source definitions.

Exclusions: this crosswalk does not resolve the Process/Event distinction within Occurrent (a separate, already-flagged open question — see Uncertainty).

**Update (2026-07-02), GFO/YAMATO/TUpper/GUM buildout:** `ADR-0015` (2026-07-02) expanded XwkOnt's source-ontology scope to include GFO, YAMATO, TUpper, and GUM. This crosswalk is the first to receive the resulting content buildout, resolving `uncertainty-004`. None of the four use "continuant/occurrent" terminology identically to BFO/DOLCE/UFO:

- **GFO** uses its own top-level pair, **Presential/Occurrent** (not "Continuant") — Occurrent is shared vocabulary with BFO/DOLCE/UFO's occurrent side, but the continuant-side term differs.
- **YAMATO** uses **Continuant/Occurrent** directly, parenthetically glossed as Object/Process, with an explicit refusal to use "perdurant."
- **TUpper** uses no continuant/occurrent-style label at all — its PSL-derived vocabulary (`object`/`activity_occurrence`) defines `object` negatively (anything that is not a timepoint, activity, or activity-occurrence).
- **GUM** uses no continuant/occurrent-style label either — its `SimpleThing`/`Process` split is grounded in systemic-functional linguistics (participant vs. verb-realized "goings-on"), not a metaphysical persistence criterion, structurally closer to this crosswalk's existing SUMO precedent than to BFO/DOLCE/UFO.

Each source's OWL/CLIF files were fetched and read directly (no PDF extraction needed for GFO or TUpper/GUM's OWL; a 2010 YAMATO technical report PDF was extracted for YAMATO since its dedicated 2022 paper and OWL/Hozo download were both inaccessible — see `note-004` and the relevant reference records for details).

**Update (2026-07-01), following direct primary-source verification of DOLCE and UFO:** the WonderWeb D18 report and the UFO 2021 Applied Ontology paper (already fetched and extracted for other crosswalks — `object.md`, `role.md`, `process.md`, `relation.md`) were reused to verify this crosswalk's DOLCE and UFO content directly, resolving `uncertainty-001`. Findings:

- **DOLCE's endurant/perdurant definitions are confirmed**, with more exact wording than the earlier secondary-source paraphrase: "Endurants are wholly present (i.e., all their proper parts are present) at any time they are present. Perdurants, on the other hand, just extend in time by accumulating different temporal parts, so that, at any time they are present, they are only partially present." D18 also **directly confirms** `note-002`'s claim about DOLCE's cognitive orientation, in the authors' own words: "we must emphasise that this distinction is motivated by our cognitive bias, and we do not commit to the fact that both these kinds of entity really exist."
- **UFO's endurant/perdurant definitions are confirmed** as originally paraphrased, now as a direct quotation: "UFO distinguishes between endurants and perdurants. Endurants are individuals that exist in time with all their parts. Perdurants are individuals that unfold in time accumulating temporal parts. An endurant can change while maintaining its identity, a perdurant cannot."
- **`uncertainty-001` (UFO's philosophical grounding) is resolved.** The 2021 paper traces UFO's origin to conceptual-modeling ontology evaluation work (Bunge-Wand-Weber), later merging with DOLCE and the General Formal Ontology (GFO) on the basis of a shared commitment to a **four-category ontology** ("the Aristotelian Square," per Lowe 2006) distinguishing individuals/types crossed with substantials/moments. This is a different philosophical lineage from both BFO's realist/scientific-practice grounding and DOLCE's cognitive/linguistic orientation — UFO's stated motivation is explicitly the practical requirements of conceptual modeling languages, informed by analytic four-category ontology, not a cognitive-bias disclaimer or a scientific-realist stance.

**Epistemic note:** BFO and SUMO content is verified directly against primary sources (`bfo-core.ttl`, `Merge.kif`). DOLCE and UFO content is now also directly verified (2026-07-01), reusing extractions performed for other crosswalks in this repository. The `xwkont:ref:borgo-galton-kutz-2022` editorial framing (BFO's "realist" and DOLCE's "cognitive/linguistic" characterizations) remains secondary-sourced and unverified — see Future Work.
