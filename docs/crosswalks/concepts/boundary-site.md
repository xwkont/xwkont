## Scope Note

**Selection rationale (per `ADR-0018`'s admission bar):** Boundary / Site is the eighth concept drafted from the `0.2.0` batch. Direct primary-source verification this session found a nameable category in **4 of 8 sources** (BFO `continuant fiat boundary`/`site`/`process boundary`, GFO `Material_boundary`, TUpper `edge`/`ridge`/`border`/`outer`, and DOLCE `feature` — this last one not in `TODO.md`'s original seed, a genuine addition). SUMO, UFO, YAMATO, and GUM were confirmed to have no reified boundary/site category. TUpper's claimed terms exist as real IRIs but, like `space.md`'s `MaterialObject`/`ShapedObject` finding, are entirely undocumented — zero `rdfs:comment` or prose gloss anywhere in the fetched OWL artifact.

This crosswalk compares each source ontology's own treatment of dependent, boundary-like, or fiat-demarcated entities — mereotopological boundaries (edges, surfaces, points) and dependent places/regions (holes, sites) that exist only in virtue of a host entity, as distinct from Mereology/Parthood/Aggregate (already drafted, `mereology-parthood-aggregate.md`, which flagged BFO's `fiat object part` as directly relevant background for this crosswalk) and from Spatial Region/Space (already drafted, `space.md`, which covers extended regions rather than lower-dimensional dependent boundaries). Sources compared: BFO, DOLCE, SUMO, UFO, GFO, YAMATO, TUpper, GUM (per `ADR-0015`'s 8-source scope).

**Cross-cutting finding, established this session:** BFO and DOLCE converge closely, despite using entirely different vocabulary, on a two-way split between *part-of-host* boundaries (BFO's `fiat point`/`line`/`surface`, demarcating a whole without being separately-existing material; DOLCE's `relevant-part`, "like a bump or an edge") and *not-part-of-host* dependent places (BFO's `site`, whose "boundaries... have locations determined in relation to some material entity"; DOLCE's `dependent-place`, "like a hole in a piece of cheese... or the shadow of a tree"). GFO's `Material_boundary` and BFO's `process boundary` each occupy a narrower, more specific niche (a boundary dependent on one specific material object; the durationless final/initial temporal part of a process, respectively) with no exact counterpart elsewhere. TUpper's `edge`/`ridge`/`border`/`outer` occupy a related mereotopological role (connected via `in`/`part` object properties to `poly`/`point`/`surface`/`box`) but cannot be compared in *content* to the other sources' definitions, since no prose gloss for any of them exists in either fetched TUpper artifact. See `note-001`, `note-002`.

Exclusions: this crosswalk does not address Mereology/Parthood/Aggregate (already drafted; BFO's `fiat object part` — a *whole-dividing* category distinct from this crosswalk's *boundary-demarcating* one — was flagged there as directly relevant background and is cross-referenced here, not re-litigated) or Spatial Region/Space (already drafted; BFO's own `spatial region` dimensional apparatus is the un-fiat-demarcated counterpart to this crosswalk's fiat boundaries).

**Core.ttl placement is deliberately left open at this `draft` stage** — see Uncertainty.
