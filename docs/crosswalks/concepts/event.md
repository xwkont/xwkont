## Scope Note

This crosswalk compares how BFO, DOLCE, UFO, and SUMO treat "Event" on the occurrent/perdurant side of each source ontology's top-level split, building directly on `docs/crosswalks/concepts/continuant-occurrent.md`. It also directly addresses that crosswalk's `uncertainty-003` (the unresolved Process/Event boundary).

**Update (2026-07-02), ADR-0015 buildout:** extended to GFO, YAMATO, TUpper, and GUM, per `ADR-0015`'s source-ontology scope expansion. GFO has no class literally named "Event" but its `Change` class explicitly glosses "instantaneous event" as one of two technical senses of change — the closest formal analog to BFO's Process Boundary / DOLCE's Achievement. YAMATO is the most explicit of the four new sources: it names "processes and events" as distinct categories directly, and states a positive criterion for the difference ("events are made of processes and while processes can change, events cannot"). TUpper's `activity_occurrence` is a token/particular occurrence of an `activity` (type), which is a different type/token distinction than continuant-occurrent, not a category split parallel to Process/Event — treated as a structural analog only, consistent with `object.md`'s TUpper precedent. GUM's `Process` class is explicitly glossed as "the linguistic construal of 'goings-on' or events," making it, unusually, the one new source whose own definition text uses the word "events" directly (alongside BFO's altLabel usage) despite not naming a separate Event class.

**Correction (2026-07-01), following direct primary-source verification:** this crosswalk originally claimed BFO's core vocabulary has no class or label named "Event" at all. That claim was **too strong** and has been corrected after fetching BFO-2020's actual primary OWL/TTL source (`bfo-core.ttl`) directly. BFO_0000015 ("process") explicitly carries `skos:altLabel "event"@en` — meaning BFO's own core release *does* register "event" as an alternate label for `Process`, not a term absent from BFO entirely. The corrected finding: **BFO treats "event" as a synonym for Process, not as a separate category** — which is still a real difference from DOLCE and UFO (where Event is a distinct category, not a synonym for Process/Perdurant-as-a-whole), but it is a different and more precise claim than "BFO doesn't use the word." SUMO's side of the asymmetry (event-like notions nested under `Process` via `IntentionalProcess`) was independently confirmed against SUMO's actual `Merge.kif` source and holds as originally stated — see Source Definitions.

Exclusions: this crosswalk does not resolve Process on its own terms (see `docs/crosswalks/concepts/process.md`) and does not attempt a full account of DOLCE's Achievement/Accomplishment mereology or UFO's event-causation theory.

**Update (2026-07-01), following direct primary-source verification of DOLCE and UFO:** the WonderWeb D18 report and UFO 2021 paper (already fetched and extracted for other crosswalks) were reused to verify this crosswalk's DOLCE and UFO Event content directly. Findings:

- **DOLCE's Achievement/Accomplishment definitions are confirmed almost verbatim**: "eventive occurrences (events) are called achievements if they are atomic, otherwise they are accomplishments," with the stative/eventive split itself defined by cumulativity ("according to whether it holds of the mereological sum of two of its instances, i.e. if it is cumulative or not").
- **UFO's Event content is confirmed** as originally paraphrased, now a direct quotation drawing on the paper's own list of theoretical contributions and its treatment of events as manifestations of endurant dispositions.
- **`note-003`'s Vendler (1957) connection for DOLCE is resolved as a confirmed negative finding, not left open.** D18's own bibliography (90 numbered references, checked directly) contains **no citation to Vendler at all**. The Event/Stative-Achievement/Accomplishment parallel to Vendler's (1957) four-way Aktionsart classification remains a plausible external observation, but it is not something Masolo et al. (2003) draw themselves — this crosswalk should stop describing it as merely "not yet confirmed" and instead record it as checked-and-absent. The same applies to `process.md`'s parallel `uncertainty-002`.

**Epistemic note:** BFO and SUMO content is verified directly against primary sources (`bfo-core.ttl`, `Merge.kif`, fetched and grepped 2026-07-01). DOLCE and UFO content is now also directly verified (2026-07-01), reusing extractions performed for other crosswalks in this repository.
