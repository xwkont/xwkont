## Scope Note

This crosswalk asks the question `TODO.md` posed directly: does `xwkont-core:InformationArtifact` (a direct child of `Entity`, described in `data/ontology/core.ttl` as XwkOnt's own "candidate support concept") correspond to anything in BFO, DOLCE, UFO, or SUMO, or is it purely XwkOnt-introduced and out of crosswalk scope?

**Finding: not purely XwkOnt-introduced.** Real analogs exist for three of four sources — but two of those three are sourced through *extension or later literature*, not the same core specification documents already cited elsewhere in this repository:

- **BFO**: no information-artifact class exists in BFO's own core release. The analog, `Information Content Entity` (ICE), comes from the **Information Artifact Ontology (IAO)** — a separate, actively maintained OBO Foundry ontology *built on* BFO (Ceusters & Smith), not part of BFO-2020 itself.
- **DOLCE**: the 2003 WonderWeb D18 deliverable (`xwkont:ref:dolce-wonderweb-d18`, cited throughout this repository's other crosswalks) does not appear to be the primary source for DOLCE's `Information Object`. That term is documented in **DOLCE+DnS Ultralite (DUL)**, the actively-maintained OWL successor to the original DOLCE Lite Plus (DLP) module bundle, built by the same DOLCE lineage (Aldo Gangemi).
- **SUMO**: has a genuine core-specification analog: `ContentBearingPhysical` (a direct subclass of `Physical`, covering both Objects and Processes that express content) and its further subclass `ContentBearingObject` (specifically for Object-like content bearers) — confirmed directly against SUMO's primary `Merge.kif` source, 2026-07-01.
- **UFO**: no distinct "Information Object" formal category was found with confidence in this pass — UFO-A treats "document" only as an example of an `Endurant`, without further formal specification found. This is recorded as a research gap, not a confirmed absence.

**Update (2026-07-01), following direct primary-source verification of BFO(IAO) and DOLCE(DUL), resolving `uncertainty-001`:** both `xwkont:ref:ceusters-smith-2015` (the IAO foundational paper) and `xwkont:ref:gangemi-dul` (the DUL OWL ontology, directly fetchable as plain-text RDF — no PDF-extraction workaround needed) were fetched and read directly.

- **BFO(IAO)'s formal ICE definition is confirmed verbatim**: "INFORMATION CONTENT ENTITY =def. an ENTITY which is (1) GENERICALLY DEPENDENT on (2) some MATERIAL ENTITY and which (3) stands in a relation of ABOUTNESS to some ENTITY." The paper also clarifies "ICEs themselves are not material entities" (only dependent on one), confirming the earlier paraphrase closely.
- **DOLCE's `InformationObject` is confirmed, with a corrected class hierarchy.** The earlier draft claimed "Information Object and Description are both subsumed by Social Object, itself a subclass of Non-Physical Object" — DUL's actual OWL source shows a different chain: `InformationObject rdfs:subClassOf InformationEntity, SocialObject` (not through any class literally named "Non-Physical Object"), where `InformationEntity` is DOLCE's abstract catchall ("a piece of information, be it concretely realized or not") and its sibling `InformationRealization` (subclass of the union of `Event`/`PhysicalObject`/`Quality`) is the concrete-realization counterpart, connected via a `realizes` relation. The underlying substance of the earlier claim (generic dependence, social/non-physical classification, independent of physical carrier) holds; the specific class-name chain has been corrected.
- **DUL is confirmed to be DOLCE Lite Plus's direct successor, not an unrelated artifact** — DUL's own header states: "The DOLCE+DnS Ultralite ontology... is a simplification of some parts of the DOLCE Lite-Plus library," authored by Aldo Gangemi (also a co-author of `xwkont:ref:dolce-borgo-2022`).
- **UFO re-checked (2026-07-01) against the already-extracted 2021 paper's full text**: zero occurrences of "document" or "information object" anywhere in the comprehensive paper. This strengthens, but does not fully resolve, `uncertainty-002` — the 2021 paper is UFO's general/comprehensive account, not the UFO-A/UFO-C papers specifically, so a genuine absence in this one paper is suggestive rather than conclusive.

**A structurally interesting parallel to XwkOnt's own posture**: `data/ontology/core.ttl` already calls `InformationArtifact` a "candidate support concept" that "may later move to metadata or provenance infrastructure" — i.e., XwkOnt itself treats this concept as peripheral to its core scaffold. BFO does the same thing architecturally: it relegates information-artifact content to a separate extension ontology (IAO) rather than its own core. That's a genuine structural echo, not just a coincidence of wording.

**Also worth noting: this is a different kind of divergence than Quality/Role/Relation's SUMO-abstracta pattern, not a repeat of it.** There, SUMO consistently chose the *abstract* branch. Here, SUMO's `ContentBearingPhysical` sits under the *Physical* branch (tying informational content directly to a physical carrier), while BFO's IAO and DOLCE's Information Object both use *generic dependence*/non-physical classification (content that can be "copied" across multiple physical carriers, independent of any one of them). So SUMO's distinctive move isn't "always abstract" — it's concept-specific, and here it goes the opposite direction from Quality/Role/Relation. This finding, and the pattern it complicates, is now written up together at `docs/methodology/sumo-modeling-patterns.md`.

**Epistemic note:** SUMO content is verified directly against its primary `Merge.kif` source. BFO(IAO) and DOLCE(DUL) content is now also directly verified (2026-07-01) against their own primary sources — `xwkont:ref:ceusters-smith-2015` (manually extracted PDF) and `xwkont:ref:gangemi-dul` (directly fetchable OWL/Turtle). UFO content remains a research gap rather than a confirmed absence, now somewhat strengthened by a full-text check of the 2021 paper — see Uncertainty and Future Work.

**Update (2026-07-02), ADR-0015 buildout:** extended to GFO, YAMATO, TUpper, and GUM. The pattern found among the original four sources — real analogs exist, but only via extension/later literature, and one source (UFO) has none with confidence — turns out to recur even more starkly among the new sources: **three of the four (GFO, TUpper, GUM) have no confident Information-Object-equivalent entity class, and one (YAMATO) has the single most developed treatment of any source examined in this crosswalk, original four included.** YAMATO's 2010 technical report devotes an entire dedicated subsection ("Ontology of informational objects") to exactly this question, works through multiple examples (a novel, a piece of music, a procedure), positively defines a `Representation`/`Representing thing` split (abstract form-and-content pair vs. its concrete physical realization — directly paralleling DOLCE(DUL)'s `InformationObject`/`InformationRealization` split), and explicitly critiques BFO's own treatment as too coarse: "BFO deals with representation (informational object) only at the highest level. It is called generically-dependent entity and it is not further elaborated what they are." GFO's `Symbol`/`Symbol_structure` classes were considered and rejected as a correspondence — they sit on GFO's `Category` (universal/type) side of its Category/Individual split, a formal-language-symbol concept (closer to a predicate or sign-type in logic) rather than a content-bearing individual artifact with aboutness, a different concept from BFO(IAO)/DOLCE(DUL)'s target. TUpper's fetched files (`tupper.all.owl`, `TUpper-Terms.html`) contain zero occurrences of "information," "content," or "represent" — confirming its already-established minimal PSL vocabulary (`object`/`activity`/`timepoint`) has no information-artifact hook at all. GUM's OWL file has no dedicated Information-Object-equivalent entity class either; its only relevant hooks, `Signification` and `Symbolization`, are *relation* types (subclasses of `Intensive`, GUM's participant-relation category), not object/entity types — a categorically different kind of thing than BFO(IAO)/DOLCE(DUL)/SUMO/YAMATO's class-level analogs.
