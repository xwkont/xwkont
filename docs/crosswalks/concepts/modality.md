## Scope Note

This crosswalk compares how the source ontologies represent modal qualification: necessity, possibility, conditionality, and volitional/non-volitional framing when those are attached to a process, state of affairs, proposition, or related bearer.

GUM is the only source here that reifies that space as a dedicated quality branch, `ModalQuality`, paired with a dedicated ascription relation, `ModalPropertyAscription`. SUMO also reifies modality, but at a different level: an alethic attribute layer on propositions (`AlethicAttribute` with `modalAttribute`, plus `Possibility` and `Necessity`). TUpper provides an explicit possibility predicate (`poss`). UFO uses modal logic substantively — a dedicated first-order modal-logic formalization section for its own axioms, plus a "modally fragile" characterization of perdurants/events — but does not introduce a dedicated modality-of-the-subject-matter class in the ontology vocabulary itself, the way GUM and SUMO do. BFO, DOLCE, GFO, and YAMATO yielded only incidental uses of "possible" / "necessary" in comments or prose, independently re-verified directly against their primary artifacts.

This draft does not re-derive `quality.md`'s `SimpleQuality` finding (`xwkont:correspondence:quality:007`, `xwkont:mapping:quality:012`) and does not re-map `disposition-capacity.md`'s `GeneralPossibility` / `Ability` findings (`xwkont:correspondence:disposition-capacity:005-006`, `xwkont:mapping:disposition-capacity:003-004`).
