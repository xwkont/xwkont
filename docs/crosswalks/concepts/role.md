## Scope Note

This crosswalk compares BFO's `Role` (a `Realizable Entity`, the other branch of `Specifically Dependent Continuant` alongside Quality), DOLCE's Role treatment (formalized via OntoClean meta-properties: anti-rigid, founded/dependent — **but see correction below: this is not part of core DOLCE**), UFO's `Role` (anti-rigid and relationally dependent, paired with `Relator`), and SUMO's `SocialRole` (a subclass of `Attribute`), completing Object's sibling pair under Continuant alongside `docs/crosswalks/concepts/quality.md`.

**Correction (2026-07-01), following direct primary-source verification:** this crosswalk originally cited DOLCE's Role treatment to `xwkont:ref:dolce-wonderweb-d18` with an acknowledged locator uncertainty. That uncertainty is now resolved, and the resolution is more significant than "which paper" — it's a scope correction. The 2022 comprehensive DOLCE paper (Borgo et al., `xwkont:ref:dolce-borgo-2022`, fetched and read directly) states explicitly: **"DOLCE does not formalize functions and roles."** What this crosswalk calls "DOLCE's Role" is actually from separate **DOLCE-driven** literature (Borgo et al., 2010; Masolo et al., 2004) — extension work built on DOLCE, not core DOLCE theory itself. This is the same kind of finding as `information-artifact.md`'s IAO/DOLCE-Lite-Plus situation: a real, citable analog exists, but it lives in extension literature, not the core specification.

**Update (2026-07-01), following direct primary-source verification of Masolo et al. (2004) itself:** this crosswalk's `uncertainty-003` (whether DOLCE-driven Role literature has a term equivalent to UFO's `Relator`) is now resolved. The 2004 paper's own apparatus — `Description`, `Concept`, `Role`, and the `classification` relation `CF(x, y, t)` — has no reified entity that mediates or acts as truthmaker for a relationship between specific bearers the way UFO's `Relator` does. Its closest construct, `Description`, instead defines a `Concept`/`Role` *in general* (a "context" or "social convention"), not a particular relationship instance; roles here are unary predicates that classify entities (`CF`), not binary relational particulars. See Source Definitions and Uncertainty for the verified picture. Separately, this pass also noticed a citation-precision issue: the "Borgo et al., 2010" citation used alongside Masolo et al. (2004) for DOLCE-driven Role literature (in both this crosswalk and `xwkont:ref:dolce-borgo-2022`'s own text) resolves to Borgo, Carrara, Garbacz & Vermaas (2010), "Formalizations of functions within the DOLCE ontology" — a paper about *functions*, not roles specifically. Role content should be attributed to Masolo et al. (2004) alone; the "Borgo et al., 2010" pairing appears to be the 2022 paper's own imprecision, not a distinct roles source this crosswalk failed to check.

**This crosswalk confirms, rather than introduces, `quality.md`'s central finding.** SUMO's `SocialRole` is a subclass of `RelationalAttribute`, itself a partition of `Attribute` — the same abstract-entity category `quality.md` found for SUMO's `Quality` analog, confirmed directly against SUMO's primary `Merge.kif` source (2026-07-01). This is not a coincidence limited to Quality: it's a systematic pattern across SUMO's whole dependent-particular family. Where BFO/DOLCE/UFO treat both Quality and Role as particulars dependent on or connected to a concrete bearer (via inherence, OntoClean meta-properties, or relational dependence), SUMO classifies both under the same abstract `Attribute` branch — SUMO's own documentation defines `SocialRole` as "the Class of all Attributes that specify the position or status of a CognitiveAgent within an Organization or other Group."

Exclusions: this crosswalk does not fully formalize OntoClean's meta-property apparatus (rigidity, identity, unity, dependence) or UFO's Relator theory beyond what's needed to place Role within them.

**Update (2026-07-02), ADR-0015 buildout:** extended to GFO, YAMATO, TUpper, and GUM — the eighth and last of the 8 existing concept crosswalks to receive the buildout. GFO has the richest Role taxonomy of any source examined in this repository: `Role` (a `Concrete` individual) subdivides into `Processual_role` (a dependent process, "roles with a process as context"), `Relational_role` (`Dependent`, `role_of` some `Relator` — directly paralleling UFO's own Role/Relator pairing found in `relation.md`), and, most strikingly, **`Social_role`, which GFO explicitly classifies as `Independent`** — `owl:disjointWith` `Dependent`. This directly contradicts the shared foundational assumption BFO, DOLCE-driven literature, and UFO all make: that a role is, by its nature, a dependent particular requiring a context or bearer. GFO's own Social_role says otherwise for at least that one role subtype. YAMATO's own text places role squarely in the dependent-particular camp instead: "The notion of quality role satisfies context-dependence which is one of the most important properties of roles. In the case of teacher role, a school is its context in which teacher role is defined" — an example directly comparable to BFO's "student role... university." YAMATO's text further states "roles are used in defining all the types in YAMATO," a more foundational role for the Role concept than any other source examined in this repository, though the paper itself defers full elaboration to a separate publication (Mizoguchi 2007, not independently fetched) and its Hozo implementation tool. TUpper again reifies nothing — "role" appears only informally inside `participates_in`'s prose gloss ("x plays some role that is not pre-specified"), never as a class. GUM's `RolePlaying` continues the same pattern already found for its Information-Artifact and Relation analogs (`information-artifact.md`, `relation.md`): it is a relation type (`rdfs:subClassOf` `Symbolization`), not an object/entity type — GUM consistently reifies this whole family of content as participant-relations rather than dependent entities.

**Epistemic note:** BFO, DOLCE, UFO, and SUMO content is now all verified directly against primary sources. BFO and SUMO: `bfo-core.ttl`, `Merge.kif` (fetched and read 2026-07-01). DOLCE: `xwkont:ref:dolce-borgo-2022` (arXiv preprint) and `xwkont:ref:masolo-vieu-2004` itself (AAAI-hosted PDF), both extracted via manual PDF-stream decompression since no PDF-text-extraction tool is otherwise available in this environment. UFO: the 2021 Applied Ontology paper's own Role and Relator passages, extracted with the same technique (reusing the extraction already performed for `object.md`/`process.md`).
