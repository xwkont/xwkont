## Scope Note

**Selection rationale (per `ADR-0018`'s admission bar):** Quantity / Amount of Matter is the fourth concept drafted from the `0.2.0` batch. Direct primary-source verification this session found a nameable category in **6 of the 8 source ontologies** (DOLCE `amount-of-matter`, SUMO `Quantity`, UFO `Quantity`, GFO `Amount_of_substrate`, YAMATO `quantity`, GUM `Quantity`) — two more sources than `TODO.md`'s seed credited (the seed listed only SUMO, DOLCE, GFO, UFO; this pass additionally found YAMATO's own elaborate quantity apparatus and GUM's `Quantity`, both previously uncredited). BFO and TUpper were confirmed to have no reified class of this kind.

This crosswalk compares each source ontology's own treatment of quantity/amount-of-matter as a category — mass-noun-denoted stuff ("gold," "water," "sand") or a measured/graded quantitative entity — distinct from Quality (already `reviewed` in `quality.md`, which covers qualitative *properties* like color or shape, not amount-like entities) and from Number/measurement machinery treated as a sub-branch where a source has one. Sources compared: BFO, DOLCE, SUMO, UFO, GFO, YAMATO, TUpper, GUM (per `ADR-0015`'s 8-source scope).

**This label covers (at least) four distinct senses across the 6 sources with content, made explicit here per a 2026-07-04 external review pass rather than left implicit in the notes below:**
1. **mass-noun stuff / amount of matter** — a physical, no-unity, mereologically-invariant category: DOLCE `amount-of-matter`, UFO `Quantity`, GFO `Amount_of_substrate` (`note-001`, `note-004`).
2. **measurement/specification quantity** — an Abstract, "how many/how much" category that need not be material (SUMO's own worked example is currency): SUMO `Quantity`/`PhysicalQuantity` (`note-001`).
3. **value-like true quantity** — a generically-dependent, unit-independent value distinct from its own measurement, closer to a quale than to mass-noun stuff: YAMATO `quantity` (`note-002`).
4. **linguistic quantity-ascription relation** — not an entity at all, but the clause-level relation of predicating a quantity of something: GUM `Quantity`/`QuantityAscription` (`note-003`).

These four senses are related by family resemblance and a shared English label, not by a single underlying commitment — this crosswalk records all four under one `xwkont:concept:quantity-amount-of-matter` identifier because `TODO.md`'s seed and `ADR-0018`'s admission bar treat them as one candidate, but the eventual core.ttl placement (`uncertainty-001`) should not assume they resolve to one class.

**Cross-cutting finding, established this session:** three of the four sources with a "physical stuff" reading of this concept (DOLCE, UFO, GFO) converge on classifying it as **physical/concrete**, alongside or under their own material-endurant-equivalent branch — DOLCE's `amount-of-matter` is a `physical-endurant`; UFO's `Quantity` is a leaf of `Substantial` (endurant-equivalent), defined as "maximally-topologically-self-connected... of homeomerous amounts of matter"; GFO's `Amount_of_substrate` is `Continuous`+`Presential` (equivalent to `Mass_entity`). **SUMO alone classifies `Quantity` as Abstract** — the opposite side of its own top-level split from where the other three sources' equivalent categories sit — a divergence structurally parallel to (though the reverse orientation of) the BFO-vs-rest pattern `time.md` found for Time. YAMATO's own "quantity" is a genuine citation-precision trap: it denotes a *quality-value*-like generically-dependent entity (closer to DOLCE's `quale`/quality-value machinery than to DOLCE's/UFO's/GFO's amount-of-matter sense), not a mass-noun-stuff category — see `note-002`. GUM's `Quantity` is a linguistic *ascription relation* (how a quantity is predicated of an entity in a clause), not an entity class at all — the same relation-not-entity pattern GUM showed for other `0.2.0`-batch concepts. See `note-001`.

Exclusions: this crosswalk does not address Quality (already `reviewed`; `quality.md` already covers the qualitative-property side of the Quality/Quantity distinction that both SUMO's and YAMATO's own documentation explicitly discuss) or Mereology/Parthood/Aggregate (a separate `0.2.0`-batch candidate; DOLCE's `amount-of-matter`'s "mereologically invariant" character and UFO's "maximally-topologically-self-connected" criterion are directly relevant background, noted below but not crosswalked here).

**Core.ttl placement is deliberately left open at this `draft` stage**, for the same reasons as the three prior `0.2.0`-batch crosswalks. No `xwkont-core:Quantity`/`AmountOfMatter` class exists yet in `data/ontology/core.ttl` — see Uncertainty.
